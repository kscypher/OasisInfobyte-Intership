Tribute to Shivaji Maharaj
(1630-1680)

The Founder Of Marathan Empire

THE PEOPLE'S KING

The Mughals ruled over Northern and Central India in the 17th century. There were countless warfare in the Deccan against kingdoms like Bijapur and Jangira. Under Mughal dominion, the Rajputs were subjugated by centuries of servitude. Common people suffered at the hands of unscrupulous police. Shivaji was born at this period. Son of a valiant warrior, Shahaji, and a religious mother, Jijabai, and a pupil of Dadoji, he immediately recognized and battled for his own people's independence. Karna's stories never fail to inspire him.

Chatrapathi Shivaji Maharaj, as he was known, assembled an unbreakable force known as the Marathan army, which tore through the Mughal army, causing Aurangzeb shrink in dread and flee. His valiant exploits encouraged the Rajputs, particularly Durgadas, who battled Aurangzeb and rescued their homeland from Aurangzeb's oppression, restoring their fugitive monarch.
